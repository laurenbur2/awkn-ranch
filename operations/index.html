Guest Checkout Checklist (Yurts & Domes)

Guest Closing Checklist

  • ! Remove all personal belongings from room, bathroom, and outdoor areas
  • ! Place used towels and linens in the laundry basket near the door
  • ~ Rinse any used dishes and place in the sink or dish bin
  • ~ Dispose of all trash in the provided waste bins (separate recycling)
  • ! Turn off all lights, fans, and AC/heater units
  • ! Close and latch all windows and the main door
  • ~ Return any borrowed items (blankets, books, yoga mats) to the welcome shelf
  • ~ Complete the digital checkout form (condition report + feedback)
  • ! Ensure no items are left in the safe or under the bed

Staff Turnover Checklist

  • ! Strip all bed linens, pillowcases, and mattress protectors
  • ! Replace with fresh linens — hospital corners, decorative pillows
  • ! Deep clean bathroom: toilet, shower, sink, mirror, floor
  • ! Restock bathroom: towels (2 bath, 2 hand, 2 wash), toiletries, TP
  • ! Vacuum/sweep all floors, mop hard surfaces
  • ! Wipe all surfaces: nightstands, desk, shelves, light switches, door handles
  • ! Restock welcome amenities: water bottles, tea, snacks, welcome card
  • ! Test HVAC, smart lock, and WiFi connectivity
  • ! Inspect for damage — report anything to ranch manager
  • ✓ Mark unit as "Ready" in system — triggers next guest notification

Target Turnover Time: 3 Hours

Two-person team per dome. Includes deep clean, full linen change, amenity restock, and quality inspection. Same-day turnovers require checkout by 11am, next check-in at 3pm. Peak season may need a third person for quality assurance walkthrough.

Section 4

Compliance & Licensing

Operating a retreat with clinical services in Texas requires navigating multiple regulatory frameworks. Missing any one of these can shut down operations or create serious liability.

⚖

Clinical / KAT Compliance

  • ! DEA Schedule III registration (medical director)
  • ! Texas Medical Board supervision agreement
  • ! Licensed pharmacy relationship for ketamine sourcing
  • ! Informed consent forms (attorney-reviewed)
  • ! HIPAA compliance: EHR system, BAAs with all vendors
  • ! Emergency protocols and crash cart / AED
  • ! Clinical SOPs documented and staff-trained
  • ~ Voluntary accreditation (JCAHO or similar)
🏢

Hospitality & Property

  • ! Short-Term Rental (STR) permit — City of Austin or Travis County
  • ! Hotel Occupancy Tax (HOT) registration — 6% state + local
  • ! Business license / DBA filing
  • ! Fire safety inspection for each lodging unit
  • ! Commercial pool permit + CPO on staff
  • ~ Commercial kitchen license (when meals go on-site)
  • ! Septic system inspection/permit for capacity
  • ~ ADA accessibility compliance (at least 1 dome)
📋

Employment & Insurance

  • ! Workers' compensation insurance
  • ! Employment practices liability (EPLI)
  • ! 1099 vs W-2 classification (audit-proof)
  • ! Background checks for all staff with guest access
  • ! Drug-free workplace policy (ironic but required for insurance)
  • ~ Non-compete / NDA for clinical staff
  • ~ Sexual harassment training (mandatory in TX for 15+ employees)

Critical: KAT Without Proper Licensing = Felony

Ketamine is a Schedule III controlled substance. Administering without a DEA-registered physician's supervision is a federal offense. The medical director must have an active DEA registration and Texas medical license. No exceptions — not even for "wellness" framing. Budget $5,000-$10,000 for initial legal review of clinical compliance.

Section 8

Critical Risk Assessment

Every risk has a mitigation. The goal isn't zero risk — it's knowing exactly what can go wrong and having a plan before it does.

High Impact

Adverse KAT Reaction

Medical emergency during a ketamine session — cardiac event, severe dissociation, psychotic episode, or allergic reaction.

Mitigation: Thorough pre-screening (cardiac history, psych eval). On-site crash cart + AED. RN present during all sessions. Written emergency protocols. Hospital within 20 minutes. Standing relationship with local ER.
High Impact

Regulatory / Licensing Failure

DEA audit, state medical board complaint, or licensing lapse that forces temporary closure of clinical operations.

Mitigation: Quarterly compliance audits. Legal counsel on retainer specializing in psychedelic medicine law. All licenses tracked with 90-day renewal reminders. Document everything.
High Impact

Wildfire / Natural Disaster

Austin's drought-prone climate and 12 acres of vegetation create real wildfire risk. Also: ice storms (Feb 2021), flooding.

Mitigation: Defensible space around structures (30 ft cleared). Fire-resistant dome/yurt materials. Evacuation plan posted in every unit. Generator for well pump. Business interruption insurance.
Medium Impact

Key Person Dependency

Medical director leaves, lead therapist burns out, or ranch manager quits — any single departure can cripple operations.

Mitigation: Cross-train staff. Maintain backup contractor relationships for each key role. Document all SOPs so anyone can step in. Non-compete agreements for clinical staff.
Medium Impact

Negative Review / PR Crisis

Guest has a bad experience, posts on social media, media picks up "ketamine retreat gone wrong" angle.

Mitigation: Excellent intake screening (say no to poor-fit guests). Post-session follow-up within 24 hours. Crisis communication plan. Media training for founders. NDA option for clinical participants.
Medium Impact

Infrastructure Failure

Septic overflow, well pump failure, internet outage, HVAC breakdown in summer — any can make the property uninhabitable.

Mitigation: Preventive maintenance schedule (septic pump every 3 years, HVAC servicing quarterly). Backup internet (cellular). Generator for essential systems. Emergency vendor contacts on speed dial.
Low Probability

Guest Injury on Property

Slip on trail, pool incident, allergic reaction to meal, snake/scorpion encounter on 12 acres of Texas hill country.

Mitigation: Liability waivers for all guests. Trail maintenance and lighting. Pool rules enforcement. First aid kits in every structure. Snake awareness signage. General liability + umbrella coverage.
Low Probability

Regulatory Change

Texas or federal government changes ketamine scheduling or restricts off-label use for therapy.

Mitigation: Diversified revenue (lodging works without KAT). Legal monitoring of legislation. Relationship with advocacy groups (MAPS, Ketamine Research Foundation). Pivot plan to other legal modalities.
