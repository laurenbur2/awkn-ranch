Exit Strategy

Year 5 Commercial Cash-Out Refinance

The primary exit mechanism is a commercial cash-out refinance at Year 5, when Phase 1 reaches stabilized NOI of $2.8M+. This is the most common and proven exit for value-add real estate sponsors — and critically, refinance proceeds are not a taxable event for investors, unlike a property sale.

Bank Underwriting Requirements (Year 5)

  • DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio): Banks require minimum 1.25x, prefer 1.35–1.50x. At $2.8M NOI, AWKN comfortably exceeds this threshold
  • Operating History: 5 years of stabilized financials — the ideal window for bank underwriting confidence
  • Trailing 12-Month NOI: Banks underwrite on actual T-12 performance, not projections. Year 5 provides verified data
  • Cap Rate Range: 8.5–9.5% for boutique experiential hospitality in Austin metro ($29M–$33M implied value)
  • LTV: 65–70% for commercial hospitality cash-out refinance (conservative vs. the 75% available for conventional CRE)
  • Rate Environment: Fixed rates of 6.5–8.0% for 5–7 year terms, 20–25 year amortization

Investor Payout Waterfall

  • Step 1: Return of original invested capital ($500K — 1x return of principal)
  • Step 2: Accrued preferred return (per operating agreement terms)
  • Step 3: Remaining proceeds distributed per profit-sharing provisions
  • Tax Advantage: Refinance proceeds are debt, not a sale — no capital gains tax triggered at the entity level
  • Operators retain ownership: Unlike a sale, the business continues generating cash flow after investor redemption

Phase 2 Supplemental Refinance (Year 7–8)

  • Once the Dome Collective stabilizes ($2.7M+ annual net), a second refinance on the combined asset generates additional liquidity
  • Combined NOI of $5.5M+ at an 8.5% cap rate implies a $64.7M combined valuation
  • Second refinance available for GP equity extraction, reinvestment, or any remaining investor obligations
  • Two-phase approach de-risks the exit — Phase 1 refinance alone is sufficient for full investor redemption

Model Assumptions

Key Assumptions & Methodology

Phase 1 — AWKN Ranch + Within Center

  • Retreat House: 10 beds, $349 (shared) – $499 (private)/night, 50% target occupancy, 2-night minimum
  • Yurts x2: $699/night each, 30% base occupancy
  • HoneyComb Dome: $599/night, 30% base occupancy
  • Lodging Domes x2: 8 beds total, $299/bed/night, 30–50% occupancy
  • Maloka Dome: $20K–$40K/mo venue rental + $12K–$18K/mo overnight capacity
  • Additional Venue Rentals: $8K–$16K/mo from temple, grounds, and event spaces
  • Within Center: $16.5K–$33K/mo from outpatient ketamine therapy (AWKN/HEAL/DISCOVER packages)
  • AWKN Hosted Retreats: $20K–$75K/mo curated multi-day immersions
  • Memberships: $3K–$8K/mo (16 members secured pre-marketing)
  • Monthly operating expenses: $60,800 (low) – $73,800 (high)
  • Months 1–3: 25–50% revenue ramp (infrastructure build-out, marketing launch)
  • Months 4–6: 65–85% of steady-state revenue (occupancy growing)
  • Months 7+: 100% steady-state revenue
  • Annual net profit targets: $2.0M (Y2) → $2.3M (Y3) → $2.55M (Y4) → $2.8M (Y5), then ~2% growth Y6–Y10
  • Experience economy split: 30% to AWKN on facilitator services, 20% on events

Phase 2 — AWKN Dome Collective

  • Adjacent 9-acre parcel, 103 geodesic dome residences total
  • 50 Tier 1 domes: $1,900/mo + $122 HOA = $2,022/mo each
  • 40 Tier 2 domes: $2,200/mo + $122 HOA = $2,322/mo each
  • 13 Short-term rental domes: $299/night at 70% occupancy
  • Gross monthly revenue at full occupancy: $275,511
  • Estimated net monthly: ~$200,000 (27% expense ratio)
  • Year 2: 30% of domes operational (construction ramp)
  • Year 3: 80% operational
  • Year 4+: 100% operational
  • Annual growth rate: 3% (lease renewals + rate increases)
  • Waitlist: 408 interested residents
  • Series A ($16M) funds land acquisition ($10M) + build-out ($6M)

General

  • All figures in USD. No inflation adjustment.
  • Expense growth: 3% annually (staffing, utilities, maintenance)
  • No additional capital raises assumed beyond Phase 1 ($500K) and Series A ($16M)
  • Conservative scenario uses low-end revenue and high-end expenses throughout
  • Base case uses midpoint revenue and midpoint expenses
  • Strong scenario uses high-end revenue and low-end expenses
  • Historical validation: Within Center has operated for 4.5 years, 1,500+ sessions, weekly sales of $20K+
